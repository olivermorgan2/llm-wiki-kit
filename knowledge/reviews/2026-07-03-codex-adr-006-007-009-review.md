1. Verdict: NEEDS_REVISION — Score 3/5

The three ADRs are directionally coherent, but two decision-level completeness
gaps block acceptance: they are semantic (not test) failures, so passing
`go test`/`check-plan` does not clear them.

2. Blocking findings

Blocking finding 1 — ADR-006 rollback claim unbacked
- ADR-006 Decision claimed interruption recovery could "roll back to the
  pre-commit state", but the staging manifest recorded only content/base
  **hashes**. Hashes prove what a file was; they cannot reconstruct overwritten,
  deleted, or metadata-modified pre-existing bytes.
- Required fix: define a rollback-capable preimage/backup model (with absent /
  content / metadata representation), or switch to roll-forward-only and drop
  the rollback claim.

Blocking finding 2 — ADR-009 ownership/version manifest underspecified
- ADR-009 named a "version-record file" but not its path, ownership class,
  minimum schema, first-install behaviour, or missing/corrupt/hash-inconsistent
  policy — leaving Phase 2 implementers without decision-level constraints to
  place, protect, or overwrite around it.
- Required fix: specify path (likely under `.llm-wiki/`), ownership class,
  minimum fields, first-install behaviour, fail-closed policy, and
  user-modified-plugin-owned-asset handling on upgrade/uninstall.

3. Non-blocking findings

- ADR-007: tighten phase-boundary language so Phase 3 owns registry/trust and
  Phase 7 owns local-path custom-profile implementation.
- ADR-006: define source/target base hashes concretely — absent-file sentinel,
  symlink/non-regular handling consistent with ADR-005.
- ADR-009: distinguish "install writes only plugin-owned assets" from "every
  installed asset is classified plugin-owned/repo-owned" (managed-asset record
  vs full repo inventory).

4. Validation assessment (as reported to Codex)

- check-plan ADR C1–C5 pass; ADR-C6 semantic-conflict remains a standing WARN.
- `sync-adr-index` clean.
- `go test ./...` PASS across 8 packages (supervisor rerun).
- Placeholder scan clean.
- Tests do not prove rollback feasibility or manifest ownership semantics, so
  they are insufficient to clear the two blockers.

5. Recommendation

Do not accept ADR-006/007/009 yet. Revise ADR-006 (rollback/preimage or
roll-forward-only) and ADR-009 (manifest path/ownership/schema/first-install/
missing-corrupt policy), then rerun the ADR checks and re-review. If no new
semantic conflicts appear, this should move to READY.
